Spaghetti Carbonara

Rich, creamy, and full of savory flavor, Spaghetti Carbonara is an iconic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper — simple yet deeply satisfying.

Preparation:

10 minutes

Cook:

15 minutes

Total:

About 25 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup (100g) grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Salt, for pasta water

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente according to package instructions. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat a pan over medium heat. Add pancetta and cook until crispy and browned. Turn off heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated cheese, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper until smooth.
  4. Immediately after draining the pasta, add it to the pan with the pancetta (off heat). Toss to combine and slightly cool (so the eggs won’t scramble).
  5. Pour the egg-cheese mixture over the hot pasta, tossing vigorously to coat. Gradually add a little reserved pasta water to create a creamy sauce, if needed.
  6. Serve immediately, topped with extra cheese and black pepper.
