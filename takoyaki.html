Takoyaki

Golden, crispy outside, soft and savory inside â€” Takoyaki are delicious Japanese octopus balls that are surprisingly fun to make at home.

Preparation:

15-20 minutes active prep

Cook:

15 minutes

Total:

About 30-35 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups (480ml) dashi stock (or water if needed)
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cooked octopus, cut into small bite-sized pieces
  • 2 green onions, finely sliced
  • 1/4 cup pickled red ginger (beni shoga), chopped
  • Vegetable oil, for greasing the pan
  • Optional toppings: takoyaki sauce, Japanese mayo, bonito flakes, aonori (seaweed flakes)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, eggs, soy sauce, and dashi stock until smooth. The batter should be very thin and runny.
  2. Preheat a takoyaki pan over medium heat and brush each hole generously with oil.
  3. Pour the batter into the pan, filling each hole to the top and letting it overflow slightly around the holes.
  4. Place a piece of octopus into each hole. Add a little green onion and pickled ginger on top of each one.
  5. As the batter starts to cook, use a skewer or chopstick to push the edges into the holes, forming rough balls.
  6. Keep turning the takoyaki balls every minute or two, allowing them to cook evenly and become golden and crisp on the outside.
  7. Cook for about 8-10 minutes, until they are firm and well-rounded.
  8. Transfer the cooked takoyaki to a plate. Drizzle with takoyaki sauce and mayonnaise. Sprinkle with bonito flakes and aonori if using.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy while the centers are still creamy.
