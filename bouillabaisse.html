Bouillabaisse

Rich, aromatic Provençal fish stew — perfect for when you want your kitchen to smell like the French Riviera!

Preparation:

30 minutes active prep

Simmer Time:

45-60 minutes

Total:

About 1.5 hours

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 leeks (white part only), sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large fennel bulb, sliced
  • 4 large tomatoes, chopped (or 1 can diced tomatoes)
  • 1 strip orange peel
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 4 cups (1 liter) fish stock or water
  • 1 pound (450g) firm white fish (like cod, snapper), cut into chunks
  • 1/2 pound (225g) shellfish (mussels, clams, shrimp)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Rouille sauce and toasted baguette slices, for serving (optional but highly recommended)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion, leeks, and garlic. Sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the fennel and tomatoes. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in the orange peel, saffron, cayenne, bay leaf, and thyme. Cook for 1 minute to release the aromas.
  4. Pour in the fish stock (or water). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes to develop the broth.
  5. Add the firm fish pieces to the simmering broth. Cook gently for about 5 minutes.
  6. Add the shellfish (mussels, clams, shrimp) and continue cooking for another 5-7 minutes until the shellfish open and shrimp are pink and cooked through.
  7. Season the bouillabaisse with salt and pepper to taste. Remove and discard the bay leaf and orange peel.
  8. Serve hot, ladling broth and seafood into bowls. Traditionally topped with a dollop of rouille and a few slices of toasted baguette.
