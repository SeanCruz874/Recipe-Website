Bulgogi

Bulgogi is a classic Korean dish made of thinly sliced beef marinated in a flavorful blend of soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and other ingredients, then grilled or stir-fried. It’s tender, savory, slightly sweet, and perfect served with rice or wrapped in lettuce.

Preparation:

15–20 minutes (plus 30 minutes marinating time)

Cook:

10–15 minutes

Total:

About 45–60 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 500g (1 lb) beef ribeye or sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 pear or apple, grated (for tenderizing)
  • 1/4 onion, grated or finely chopped
  • 1/2 onion, sliced (for cooking)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine or mirin (optional)
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Vegetable oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, grated pear, grated onion, and optional honey or rice wine. Mix well.
  2. Add sliced beef to the marinade, mix to coat evenly, and let marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to overnight in the fridge).
  3. Heat a lightly oiled skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Add the marinated beef and sliced onions.
  4. Cook for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the beef is fully cooked and slightly caramelized.
  5. Sprinkle with green onions and sesame seeds before serving.
  6. Serve with steamed rice, or wrap in lettuce with ssamjang and sliced garlic.
