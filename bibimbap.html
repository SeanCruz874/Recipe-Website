Bibimbap

Bibimbap is a vibrant Korean rice dish topped with assorted sautéed vegetables, seasoned beef, fried egg, and gochujang (chili paste). It’s colorful, nutritious, and packed with flavor — served in a bowl and mixed together before eating.

Preparation:

30–40 minutes

Cook:

15 minutes

Total:

About 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • 200g (7 oz) ground beef or sliced beef (e.g., ribeye)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup spinach, blanched and seasoned with sesame oil and salt
  • 1 small carrot, julienned and sautéed
  • 1/2 zucchini, julienned and sautéed
  • 1 cup bean sprouts, blanched and seasoned
  • 4–5 shiitake mushrooms, sliced and sautéed
  • 1 egg (per serving), fried sunny-side-up
  • Gochujang (Korean red chili paste), to taste
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Vegetable oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine beef with soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and sugar. Marinate for 15–20 minutes.
  2. Cook each vegetable separately in a lightly oiled pan with a pinch of salt, just until tender. Set aside.
  3. Cook the marinated beef in a skillet over medium heat until browned and cooked through.
  4. Fry eggs sunny-side-up, keeping the yolk runny if desired.
  5. To assemble: Place a mound of rice in a large bowl. Arrange the vegetables and beef around the top in sections. Place the fried egg in the center.
  6. Add a spoonful of gochujang on top. Sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  7. Mix thoroughly before eating and enjoy!
