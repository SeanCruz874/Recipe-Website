Green Curry

Thai Green Curry is a rich, aromatic dish made with creamy coconut milk, green curry paste, tender chicken, and vibrant vegetables. Balanced with the flavors of basil, lime, and fish sauce, it’s a warm and spicy comfort food perfect with steamed jasmine rice.

Preparation:

10–15 minutes

Cook:

20–25 minutes

Total:

30–40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 400ml (1 can) coconut milk
  • 250g (9 oz) chicken breast or thigh, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup eggplant or zucchini, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced (optional)
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots (optional)
  • 1–2 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian)
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup Thai basil leaves (or regular basil)
  • 4–5 kaffir lime leaves, torn (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Steamed jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add green curry paste and stir for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add half the coconut milk and stir until well combined. Let it simmer and reduce slightly.
  3. Add the sliced chicken and cook until no longer pink, about 4–5 minutes.
  4. Pour in the remaining coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.
  5. Add the vegetables (eggplant, bell pepper, bamboo shoots) and cook for 5–7 minutes until tender.
  6. Season with fish sauce and sugar. Add kaffir lime leaves if using. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  7. Stir in Thai basil just before serving.
  8. Serve hot over steamed jasmine rice and enjoy the balance of spicy, creamy, and herbal flavors.
