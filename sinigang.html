Sinigang

Bright, tangy, and packed with hearty vegetables â€” Sinigang is a comforting Filipino sour soup, perfect for cozy rainy days or a refreshing, light meal any time of year.

Preparation:

15-20 minutes active prep

Cook:

45 minutes

Total:

About 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds (680g) pork belly, pork ribs, or shrimp (can substitute beef or fish)
  • 8 cups (2 liters) water
  • 1 packet (about 40g) sinigang mix (usually tamarind flavor), or 1/2 cup fresh tamarind pulp
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1-2 long green chili peppers (siling haba)
  • 1 cup daikon radish, sliced
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup okra, whole or halved
  • 1-2 cups water spinach (kangkong) or bok choy
  • Salt and fish sauce (patis), to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the pork (or chosen protein) and simmer over medium heat, skimming off any scum that rises to the surface.
  2. Add the onion, tomatoes, and green chili peppers. Simmer for about 20-30 minutes until the meat is tender.
  3. Add the daikon radish and continue simmering for another 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the eggplant and okra, cooking until they are just tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Mix in the sinigang mix (or fresh tamarind pulp) and adjust seasoning with fish sauce and salt to your taste.
  6. Add the kangkong or bok choy last and cook for another 2-3 minutes until wilted.
  7. Serve hot over steamed rice, enjoying the broth and vegetables together.
