Croquetas

Croquetas are crispy, golden-fried bites with a creamy béchamel interior, often filled with ham (jamón), chicken, or cheese. These Spanish tapas are beloved for their rich flavor and satisfying crunch.

Preparation:

30 minutes (plus chilling time)

Cook:

20–25 minutes

Total:

About 1 hour (excluding chilling)

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup (60g) all-purpose flour (plus more for coating)
  • 2 cups (480ml) whole milk, warm
  • 3/4 cup finely chopped jamón serrano or cooked chicken
  • Salt, to taste
  • Nutmeg (optional), to taste
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 1/2 cups (150g) breadcrumbs
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, heat the butter and olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until soft and translucent.
  2. Stir in the flour and cook for 1–2 minutes to form a roux.
  3. Gradually add the warm milk, whisking constantly until the mixture thickens into a smooth béchamel sauce.
  4. Add the chopped jamón or chicken. Season with salt and a pinch of nutmeg if using. Cook for a few more minutes, then remove from heat.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a shallow dish, cover, and chill for at least 2 hours or until firm enough to shape.
  6. Once firm, shape the mixture into small logs or balls using your hands or spoons.
  7. Coat each croqueta lightly in flour, then dip in beaten egg, and roll in breadcrumbs until well coated.
  8. Heat oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C). Fry the croquetas in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 2–3 minutes per side.
  9. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.
