Kimchi Jjigae

Kimchi Jjigae is a hearty and spicy Korean stew made with fermented kimchi, pork, tofu, and vegetables. It's a warming dish perfect for cold days, known for its bold flavors and comforting richness.

Preparation:

10–15 minutes

Cook:

30 minutes

Total:

About 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups well-fermented kimchi (cut into bite-size pieces)
  • 200g (7 oz) pork belly or shoulder, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 block tofu, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups water or anchovy broth
  • 1 green onion, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce (optional, to taste)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, heat sesame oil over medium heat. Add pork and sauté until lightly browned.
  2. Add the kimchi and cook for 3–5 minutes to release flavor.
  3. Stir in gochugaru, gochujang, garlic, and onion. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in water or broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. Add tofu cubes and simmer for an additional 5–10 minutes.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with soy sauce if needed.
  7. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot with rice.
