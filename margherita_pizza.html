Margherita Pizza

Simple, vibrant, and authentically Italian, Margherita Pizza celebrates the pure flavors of tomato, mozzarella, and basil — a timeless classic that's easy to love and perfect for pizza night.

Preparation:

15 minutes (plus 1 hour dough resting time)

Cook:

10–12 minutes

Total:

About 1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball pizza dough (homemade or store-bought, about 250g)
  • 1/2 cup crushed San Marzano tomatoes (or other quality canned tomatoes)
  • 100g (3.5 oz) fresh mozzarella, torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 1–2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • All-purpose flour or semolina, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 475–500°F (245–260°C). If using a pizza stone or steel, place it in the oven while preheating.
  2. On a lightly floured surface, stretch or roll out the pizza dough into a 10–12 inch circle.
  3. Spread the crushed tomatoes evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt.
  4. Top with torn mozzarella pieces and a few fresh basil leaves. Drizzle lightly with olive oil.
  5. Carefully transfer the pizza to a preheated baking sheet, pizza stone, or steel. Bake for 10–12 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling.
  6. Remove from the oven, garnish with additional fresh basil and a final drizzle of olive oil. Slice and serve hot.
