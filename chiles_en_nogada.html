Chiles en Nogada

Chiles en Nogada is a traditional Mexican dish made with poblano peppers stuffed with a savory-sweet picadillo, topped with a creamy walnut sauce (nogada), fresh parsley, and pomegranate seeds. Served chilled or at room temperature, it is a patriotic dish often enjoyed during Mexican Independence celebrations.

Preparation:

45 minutes (plus soaking time for walnuts)

Cook:

45 minutes

Total:

1 hour 30 minutes (plus soaking)

Ingredients:

  • 6 large poblano peppers
  • 1 lb (450g) ground pork or beef
  • 1/2 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 apple, peeled and diced
  • 1 ripe plantain or banana, diced
  • 1 peach or pear, peeled and diced
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup slivered almonds
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • For the Nogada (walnut sauce):
  • 1 cup walnuts (soaked overnight and peeled)
  • 1/2 cup milk or cream
  • 1/4 cup fresh cheese (queso fresco or ricotta)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • Pinch of cinnamon
  • Salt to taste
  • For garnish:
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate seeds
  • Fresh parsley leaves

Instructions:

  1. Roast the poblano peppers over an open flame or under a broiler until charred on all sides. Place in a covered bowl or plastic bag to steam, then peel the skin. Carefully make a slit and remove seeds, keeping the peppers whole.
  2. In a skillet, heat oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant. Add the ground meat and cook until browned.
  3. Add the chopped apple, plantain, peach, raisins, almonds, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Cook for 10–15 minutes until the mixture is fragrant and the fruit has softened. Let cool slightly.
  4. Carefully stuff each poblano pepper with the picadillo filling. Set aside.
  5. In a blender, combine soaked and peeled walnuts, milk or cream, cheese, cinnamon, sugar (if using), and a pinch of salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  6. Place stuffed peppers on a serving plate. Pour the walnut sauce generously over each chile.
  7. Garnish with pomegranate seeds and parsley leaves. Serve chilled or at room temperature.
