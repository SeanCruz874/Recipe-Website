Coq au Vin

A legendary French dish where tender chicken is braised in red wine with bacon, mushrooms, and herbs â€” pure comfort food with boujee energy!

Preparation:

20 minutes

Cook:

1 hour 30 minutes

Total:

About 2 hours

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 kg (about 3 lbs) chicken pieces (thighs, legs, or a whole chicken cut up)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 150g (about 5 oz) bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 2 cups (480ml) dry red wine (Pinot Noir or Burgundy is *chef's kiss*)
  • 1 cup (240ml) chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Fresh thyme sprigs
  • 200g (about 7 oz) mushrooms, halved
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a heavy pot. Fry the bacon until crispy, then remove it and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, brown the chicken pieces on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add onions, carrots, and garlic to the pot. Cook until softened.
  5. Sprinkle in the flour and stir for 1-2 minutes (to thicken the sauce later).
  6. Pour in the wine, chicken broth, and tomato paste. Stir it up and scrape the bottom of the pot to get all those yummy brown bits.
  7. Return the chicken and bacon to the pot. Add the bay leaf and thyme.
  8. Cover and simmer on low for about 1 hour and 15 minutes.
