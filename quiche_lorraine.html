Quiche Lorraine

Rich, savory custard packed with crispy bacon and gooey cheese — a French classic that's perfect for brunch dreams!

Preparation:

25 minutes active prep

Chill Time (for dough, optional but best):

30 minutes

Cook:

40-45 minutes

Total:

About 1.5 hours

Ingredients:

  • 1 9-inch pie crust (homemade or store-bought)
  • 6 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 1/4 cups (300ml) heavy cream
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup (100g) grated Gruyère cheese (or Swiss cheese)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Pinch of nutmeg (optional but fancy)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out your pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch tart pan or pie dish. Prick the bottom with a fork. Chill in the fridge for 30 minutes if you have time (helps prevent shrinking!).
  3. While the crust chills, cook the chopped bacon in a skillet over medium heat until crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. In the bacon fat, sauté the chopped onion until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  5. In a medium bowl, whisk together the cream, eggs, a pinch of salt, pepper, and a little nutmeg if using.
  6. Sprinkle the cooked bacon, sautéed onion, and grated cheese evenly over the bottom of the crust.
  7. Pour the custard mixture over the fillings.
  8. Bake for 40-45 minutes, until the quiche is puffed, golden, and just slightly wobbly in the center.
  9. Let cool for at least 10-15 minutes before slicing. (It sets as it cools — and tastes even better slightly warm or at room temp!)
  10. Slice, serve, and pretend you’re lounging at a café in Paris.
