Chow Mein

Chow Mein is a classic Chinese stir-fried noodle dish made with wheat noodles, vegetables, and optional protein such as chicken, beef, or shrimp. It’s quick to make and full of savory, umami-packed flavor with just the right hint of crispiness.

Preparation:

15–20 minutes

Cook:

10–12 minutes

Total:

25–30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 200g (7 oz) fresh or dried chow mein noodles (egg noodles)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Optional: 150g (5 oz) cooked chicken, beef, tofu, or shrimp
  • Sauce:
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce (for color, optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions:

  1. Cook noodles according to package instructions until just tender. Drain and toss with sesame oil to prevent sticking. Set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, mix all sauce ingredients until combined.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add garlic and onion, stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  4. Add vegetables and optional protein. Stir-fry 2–3 minutes until crisp-tender.
  5. Add the noodles and pour in the sauce. Toss everything together and stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes until heated through and coated.
  6. Garnish with chopped green onions. Serve hot.
