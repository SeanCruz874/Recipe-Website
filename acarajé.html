Acarajé

Crispy deep-fried black-eyed pea fritters, split open and stuffed with spicy shrimp filling — an iconic taste of Afro-Brazilian street food!

Preparation:

30 minutes (plus soaking time)

Cook:

30 minutes

Total:

About 1 hour (plus soaking)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups (about 400g) dried black-eyed peas
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying (traditionally dendê oil, aka palm oil, for authentic flavor)

For the filling (Vatapá or shrimp filling):

  • 200g (about 7 oz) small cooked shrimp (or dried shrimp if you wanna go fully traditional)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ground peanuts or cashews
  • 1 tablespoon ground dried shrimp (optional for extra umami)
  • 1 tablespoon dendê oil (or substitute vegetable oil if needed)
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) coconut milk
  • 1 slice of bread, soaked in coconut milk (optional, for thickening)
  • Salt, pepper, and chili pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak the black-eyed peas overnight. Rub them between your hands to loosen the skins, then rinse and remove as much of the skins as you can (this part is a little tedious but worth it!).
  2. Blend the peeled black-eyed peas with the chopped onion and salt until it forms a thick, smooth batter.
  3. Heat oil (or dendê oil) in a deep pan or fryer to about 350°F (175°C).
  4. Scoop big spoonfuls of the batter and carefully drop them into the hot oil. Fry until golden brown and crispy, flipping as needed (about 5-6 minutes).
  5. Drain on paper towels.
  6. Meanwhile, make the filling: sauté the onion in dendê oil until soft, then add shrimp, ground nuts, optional dried shrimp, and coconut milk. Stir and cook until thick and flavorful. You can blend it a little if you want it smoother!
  7. Slice the acarajés open (like a pita pocket), stuff them with the shrimp filling, and serve hot!
