Moqueca

A warm, tropical Brazilian fish stew simmered with coconut milk, peppers, and fresh herbs — pure comfort in a bowl!

Preparation:

20 minutes

Cook:

30-35 minutes

Total:

50-55 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 500g (about 1 lb) firm white fish (like cod, halibut, or snapper), cut into chunks
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or dendê oil (palm oil, if you have it for authentic flavor!)
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Optional: 1 chili pepper, sliced, for some heat

Instructions:

  1. Season the fish chunks with lime juice, salt, and pepper. Let it marinate while you prep the veggies.
  2. In a large deep skillet or pot, heat the oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the onions, bell peppers, and garlic, and cook until they start to soften (about 5 minutes).
  4. Layer the tomatoes over the veggies, then gently place the fish pieces on top.
  5. Pour the coconut milk evenly over everything. If you like it spicy, throw in some sliced chili pepper here too!
  6. Cover and let it simmer gently for about 20-25 minutes, until the fish is cooked through and tender (don’t stir too much or the fish will break apart!).
  7. Sprinkle with lots of chopped cilantro before serving.
  8. Serve hot, usually with rice and maybe some farofa on the side!
