Mac and Cheese

Creamy, cheesy, and comforting — Mac and Cheese is a beloved classic that brings together tender pasta and a rich, velvety cheese sauce. It’s perfect as a main dish or a hearty side.

Preparation:

10–15 minutes

Cook:

20 minutes

Total:

35 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces (225g) elbow macaroni
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk (preferably whole milk)
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika or mustard powder (optional)
  • Breadcrumbs (optional, for baked version)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the macaroni according to package directions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Stir in the flour and cook for about 1 minute, whisking constantly to form a roux.
  3. Slowly whisk in the milk and continue cooking, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens — about 5 minutes.
  4. Reduce heat to low and stir in the shredded cheese until melted and smooth. Season with salt, pepper, and optional spices.
  5. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir until evenly coated. Serve warm as is, or continue to the next step for a baked version.
  6. (Optional) Transfer to a greased baking dish, sprinkle with breadcrumbs, and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 15–20 minutes until golden and bubbly.
