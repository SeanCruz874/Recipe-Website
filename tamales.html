Tamales

Tamales are a beloved Mexican dish made from masa (corn dough) filled with meats, cheese, or vegetables, then wrapped in corn husks and steamed. Often enjoyed during holidays and celebrations, they are both comforting and festive.

Preparation:

1–1.5 hours (including soaking and dough preparation)

Cook:

1.5–2 hours steaming

Total:

2.5–3.5 hours

Ingredients:

  • 30 dried corn husks (plus extras in case of tearing)
  • 4 cups masa harina
  • 2.5–3 cups warm chicken broth or water
  • 1.5 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup lard or vegetable shortening, whipped
  • 3–4 cups filling of choice (shredded pork with red chile sauce, green chicken, cheese and jalapeño, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Soak the corn husks in warm water for 30–60 minutes until soft and pliable. Drain and pat dry.
  2. In a large bowl, mix masa harina, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add warm broth, mixing until a dough forms. Beat in whipped lard or shortening until light and fluffy. Dough should be soft but not sticky.
  3. To assemble, spread about 2 tablespoons of masa onto the center of a husk, leaving space on the sides and top. Add about 1 tablespoon of filling in the center.
  4. Fold the sides of the husk inward, then fold the bottom up to seal. Tie with a strip of husk if needed. Repeat with remaining ingredients.
  5. Arrange tamales upright in a steamer basket, open end up. Cover with extra husks or a damp towel. Steam over gently boiling water for 1.5 to 2 hours, adding water as needed to prevent drying out.
  6. Check for doneness by peeling one open — the masa should pull away from the husk cleanly and be fully cooked through.
  7. Let rest for a few minutes before serving. Serve with salsa, crema, or your favorite toppings.
