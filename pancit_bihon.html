Pancit Bihon

Light, savory, and filled with vegetables and meat — Pancit Bihon is a classic Filipino noodle dish that's quick to make and perfect for celebrations, potlucks, or a cozy meal at home.

Preparation:

15-20 minutes active prep

Cook:

20 minutes

Total:

About 40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces (225g) bihon (rice stick noodles)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1/2 pound (225g) chicken breast or thigh, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 pound (115g) pork, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined (optional)
  • 1 1/2 cups cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1/2 cup green beans, sliced thinly
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 2 cups chicken broth or water (plus more as needed)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Calamansi or lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Soak the bihon noodles in warm water for about 10 minutes, or until softened. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large wok or pan over medium heat. Sauté garlic and onions until fragrant and softened.
  3. Add the chicken (and pork if using). Stir-fry until lightly browned and cooked through. Add shrimp and cook just until pink (if using).
  4. Add the carrots, green beans, and cabbage. Stir-fry for a few minutes until vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth, soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Add the soaked bihon noodles. Toss well to combine, letting the noodles absorb the liquid. Add more broth or water if needed to fully soften the noodles without drying out.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Toss everything together until evenly mixed and heated through.
  8. Transfer to a serving platter and serve hot with calamansi or lemon wedges on the side.
