Pão de Queijo

Chewy, cheesy, and addictively delicious Brazilian cheese bread — perfect for snacks, breakfast, or anytime you need cheesy goodness!

Preparation:

15 minutes

Cook:

20-25 minutes

Total:

35-40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup (240ml) whole milk
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) vegetable oil or butter
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups (250g) tapioca flour (also called tapioca starch)
  • 1 1/2 cups (150g) grated Parmesan cheese (or a mix of Parmesan and mozzarella)
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the milk, oil, and salt over medium heat until it just starts to bubble.
  3. Remove from heat and immediately add the tapioca flour, stirring well until it forms a sticky dough.
  4. Let it cool for a few minutes, then mix in the eggs one at a time, fully incorporating each before adding the next.
  5. Stir in the grated cheese until the dough is smooth and stretchy.
  6. Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of the dough and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 20–25 minutes, or until the pão de queijo are puffed up and lightly golden on top.
  8. Enjoy warm! (They're best fresh but can be reheated later too!)
