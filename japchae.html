Japchae

Japchae is a beloved Korean noodle dish made from sweet potato starch noodles stir-fried with a colorful mix of vegetables and seasoned beef. Lightly sweet and savory, it’s served warm or at room temperature, making it perfect for any occasion.

Preparation:

20–25 minutes

Cook:

15–20 minutes

Total:

35–45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 200g (7 oz) sweet potato noodles (dangmyeon)
  • 150g (5 oz) beef (ribeye or sirloin), thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (for beef)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (for beef)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup spinach, blanched and squeezed
  • 4–5 shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 eggs, beaten and cooked into a thin omelet, sliced into strips (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (for seasoning)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (for noodles)
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Vegetable oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Marinate beef with 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 teaspoon sesame oil, and minced garlic. Set aside for 10–15 minutes.
  2. Cook sweet potato noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse with cold water. Toss with 1 tablespoon sesame oil to prevent sticking.
  3. Individually stir-fry the vegetables in a lightly oiled pan with a pinch of salt, then set aside.
  4. Cook the marinated beef until fully browned and cooked through. Set aside.
  5. In a large pan or bowl, combine noodles, cooked vegetables, beef, soy sauce, and sugar. Toss together over low heat or by hand until everything is evenly mixed and warmed.
  6. Top with egg strips (if using) and sesame seeds before serving.
  7. Serve warm or at room temperature.
