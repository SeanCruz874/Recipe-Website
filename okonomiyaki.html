Okonomiyaki

Fluffy, savory Japanese pancakes loaded with cabbage, topped with sweet and tangy sauce. Easy, customizable, and seriously satisfying.

Preparation:

15 minutes active prep

Cook:

15-20 minutes

Total:

About 30-35 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • 2/3 cup (160ml) water or dashi (Japanese broth)
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 cups (about 250g) finely shredded cabbage
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Optional fillings: cooked bacon, shrimp, squid, cheese, or whatever you like
  • Vegetable oil, for cooking
  • Okonomiyaki sauce (or a mix of ketchup, Worcestershire, and soy sauce)
  • Japanese mayo (or regular mayo)
  • Optional toppings: bonito flakes, aonori (seaweed flakes), extra green onions

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and water (or dashi) until smooth. Add the eggs and stir until just combined.
  2. Fold in the shredded cabbage, green onions, and any additional fillings you like.
  3. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly oil it.
  4. Scoop about 1 cup of the batter onto the pan, shaping it into a thick, round pancake.
  5. Cook for about 4-5 minutes until the bottom is golden brown. Flip carefully and cook another 4-5 minutes until cooked through.
  6. If using bacon, you can lay strips on top before flipping the first time, letting them cook into the pancake.
  7. Transfer to a plate and drizzle generously with okonomiyaki sauce and mayo.
  8. Sprinkle with bonito flakes, aonori, or extra green onions if you like.
  9. Slice into quarters and serve hot.
