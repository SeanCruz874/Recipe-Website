Masala Dosa

Thin, crispy, and packed with flavorful spiced potatoes — Masala Dosa is a beloved South Indian dish that's perfect for breakfast, brunch, or any time you crave something deliciously savory.

Preparation:

30 minutes active prep (plus batter fermenting time)

Ferment Time:

8-12 hours (overnight)

Cook:

30-40 minutes

Total:

About 9-13 hours (mostly inactive time)

Ingredients:

For the Dosa Batter:

  • 1 1/2 cups (300g) rice
  • 1/2 cup (100g) split urad dal (skinned black lentils)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil or ghee, for cooking

For the Potato Filling (Masala):

  • 3-4 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled, and roughly mashed
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately a few times. Soak the rice and dal separately for at least 4-6 hours.
  2. Drain the soaked dal and blend it into a smooth, fluffy batter with a little water. Do the same for the rice (it should be slightly grainy). Mix both batters together, add salt, and allow it to ferment overnight in a warm place until bubbly and slightly risen.
  3. For the filling, heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds; when they start to pop, add curry leaves, green chilies, and ginger.
  4. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they are soft and translucent.
  5. Stir in the turmeric, then add the mashed potatoes and mix well. Season with salt and garnish with chopped cilantro. Set aside.
  6. Heat a nonstick or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center and spread it outward in a thin, circular motion to make a large dosa.
  7. Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until the bottom is golden and crisp; no need to flip.
  8. Place a portion of the potato filling in the center and fold the dosa over it.
  9. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
