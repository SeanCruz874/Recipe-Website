Churros

Churros are crispy, golden sticks of fried dough coated in cinnamon sugar and often served with rich chocolate sauce. A popular street treat in Spain and Latin America, churros are easy to make at home and perfect for a sweet indulgence.

Preparation:

15 minutes

Cook:

20 minutes

Total:

35 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 2½ tablespoons sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Oil for frying (vegetable or canola)
  • ½ cup sugar + 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (for coating)

Optional Chocolate Sauce:

  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 4 oz dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, salt, and oil. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then remove from heat and stir in flour until a ball forms.
  2. Let the dough cool slightly, then transfer to a piping bag fitted with a large star tip.
  3. Heat oil in a deep skillet or pot to 350–375°F (175–190°C).
  4. Pipe 4–6 inch strips of dough into the hot oil, cutting with scissors. Fry in batches until golden brown, turning occasionally, about 2–3 minutes per side.
  5. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Roll warm churros in the cinnamon sugar mixture.
  6. For chocolate sauce, heat cream in a small saucepan until just simmering. Remove from heat, add chocolate, and stir until smooth. Add sugar and salt to taste.
  7. Serve churros warm with chocolate sauce for dipping.
