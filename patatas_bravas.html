Patatas Bravas

Patatas Bravas is one of Spain's most beloved tapas dishes — crispy fried potatoes topped with a bold, spicy tomato sauce and sometimes a creamy aioli. It’s a perfect appetizer or side for any Spanish-themed meal.

Preparation:

15 minutes

Cook:

25–30 minutes

Total:

About 40–45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds (700g) potatoes, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (plus more for frying or roasting)
  • Salt, to taste
  • For the Bravas Sauce:
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional Aioli (for topping):
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 garlic clove, finely minced
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C) or heat oil in a deep fryer to 350°F (175°C) if frying.
  2. Toss the potato cubes with oil and salt, then bake for 25–30 minutes until golden and crispy, flipping halfway. If frying, cook in batches until golden and drain on paper towels.
  3. Meanwhile, prepare the bravas sauce. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a saucepan. Add onion and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  4. Stir in smoked paprika, cayenne, and tomato paste. Cook for 1 minute.
  5. Add crushed tomatoes, sugar, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened.
  6. For the optional aioli, mix mayonnaise, minced garlic, and lemon juice in a bowl. Set aside.
  7. Transfer crispy potatoes to a serving dish. Spoon the warm bravas sauce over them and drizzle with aioli if using.
  8. Serve immediately while hot and crispy.
