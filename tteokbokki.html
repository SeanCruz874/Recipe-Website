Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean street food made with chewy rice cakes simmered in a spicy, sweet, and savory gochujang-based sauce. It’s comforting, bold, and often enjoyed with fish cakes, boiled eggs, and green onions.

Preparation:

10 minutes

Cook:

15–20 minutes

Total:

25–30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 500g (1 lb) Korean rice cakes (tteok)
  • 150g (5 oz) Korean fish cakes, sliced
  • 2 cups water or anchovy broth
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 boiled egg (optional, per serving)
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Soak rice cakes in warm water for 10–15 minutes if they’re hard or refrigerated.
  2. In a pan, combine water (or broth), gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, and garlic. Stir and bring to a boil.
  3. Add rice cakes and fish cakes to the sauce. Stir gently and simmer over medium heat for 10–15 minutes, until the sauce thickens and the rice cakes are soft and chewy.
  4. Add boiled eggs (if using) and cook for another 2–3 minutes to warm through.
  5. Sprinkle with chopped green onion and sesame seeds before serving.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy immediately!
