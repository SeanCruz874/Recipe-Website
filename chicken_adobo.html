Chicken Adobo

Savory, tangy, and rich with a hint of sweetness â€” Chicken Adobo is a beloved Filipino classic that's easy to make yet full of bold, comforting flavors, perfect for any meal.

Preparation:

10-15 minutes active prep

Marinate Time:

30 minutes (optional, for deeper flavor)

Cook:

40-50 minutes

Total:

About 1.5 hours (with marinating)

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds (900g) bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) cane vinegar or white vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns (or cracked black pepper)
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional, for a slight sweetness)
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) water (as needed)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Steamed rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Toss to coat and marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for deeper flavor.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and brown the pieces on all sides.
  3. Once browned, pour the reserved marinade over the chicken. Add water if needed to just cover the chicken. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is tender and the sauce is slightly reduced.
  5. Uncover the pot, add brown sugar (if using), and continue to simmer uncovered for another 10 minutes to thicken the sauce further, spooning it over the chicken occasionally.
  6. Adjust seasoning if needed. Discard the bay leaves before serving.
  7. Serve hot with plenty of steamed rice to soak up the delicious sauce.
