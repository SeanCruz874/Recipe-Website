Biryani

Fragrant, spiced, and full of layers of flavor — Chicken Biryani is a festive dish that's perfect for special occasions but can also elevate any weekend meal.

Preparation:

30-40 minutes active prep

Marinate Time:

1 hour (or overnight for deeper flavor)

Cook:

45-60 minutes

Total:

About 2.5 hours (including marinating)

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds (680g) boneless or bone-in chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup (240g) plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 cups (400g) basmati rice, rinsed well and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 1/2 cups (600ml) water or chicken broth
  • Fresh cilantro and mint leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Saffron threads (optional), soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the yogurt, lemon juice, cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, chili powder, and salt. Add the chicken and coat well. Cover and marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight in the fridge.
  2. In a large, heavy pot or Dutch oven, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring often, until deep golden brown and caramelized. Remove half of the onions and set aside for garnishing.
  3. To the remaining onions in the pot, add the garlic, ginger, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and bay leaf. Cook for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the marinated chicken along with all the marinade. Cook for about 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken starts to brown and the spices become aromatic.
  5. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot in an even layer over the chicken. Pour in the water or chicken broth carefully. Do not stir. Sprinkle the saffron milk (if using) over the top.
  6. Bring the pot to a simmer over medium heat. Once simmering, cover with a tight-fitting lid, reduce heat to low, and cook for about 20–25 minutes until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Turn off the heat and let the biryani sit, covered, for another 10 minutes to steam.
  8. Fluff gently with a fork, garnish with the reserved caramelized onions, cilantro, and mint. Serve hot with raita or a side salad if desired.
