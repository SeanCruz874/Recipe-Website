Mapo Tofu

Mapo Tofu is a classic Sichuan dish made with silky tofu, ground pork or beef, and a spicy, numbing sauce featuring doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste) and Sichuan peppercorns. It’s bold, flavorful, and perfect with a bowl of steamed rice.

Preparation:

10–15 minutes

Cook:

15 minutes

Total:

25–30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 400g (14 oz) soft or medium-firm tofu, cubed
  • 150g (5 oz) ground pork or beef
  • 2 tablespoons doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste)
  • 1 tablespoon fermented black beans (optional), rinsed and chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup chicken or vegetable stock (or water)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (slurry)
  • 1 teaspoon ground Sichuan peppercorns (adjust to taste)
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok or pan over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Add ground pork or beef and cook until browned.
  3. Stir in doubanjiang and fermented black beans (if using). Cook for 1–2 minutes to release the flavor.
  4. Add soy sauce, sugar, and stock. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Gently add the tofu cubes. Simmer for 4–5 minutes, allowing the tofu to absorb flavor.
  6. Stir in the cornstarch slurry and gently mix to thicken the sauce.
  7. Sprinkle with ground Sichuan peppercorns and chopped green onions before serving.
  8. Serve hot over steamed rice.
