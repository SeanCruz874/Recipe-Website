Tempura

Light, crispy, golden-fried magic — Tempura is crunchy perfection that's way easier to make than you think!

Preparation:

20 minutes active prep

Cook:

20 minutes

Total:

About 40 minutes (and every bite is pure joy!)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour (plus a little extra for dusting)
  • 1 egg
  • 3/4 cup (180ml) ice-cold water
  • Vegetables: sweet potato slices, zucchini, mushrooms, bell pepper strips, etc.
  • Seafood: shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • Vegetable oil, for deep frying
  • Optional dipping sauce: soy sauce + mirin + a little sugar (or just straight soy sauce!)

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pot to about 340-360°F (170-180°C). (If you don't have a thermometer, test with a small drop of batter — it should sizzle immediately but not burn!)
  2. In a bowl, beat the egg gently. Add the ice-cold water (it needs to be really cold!).
  3. Add the flour to the egg mixture and stir very lightly with chopsticks or a fork — it’s okay if it's a little lumpy. (Overmixing = sad, heavy tempura.)
  4. Lightly dust your veggies and shrimp with flour before dipping them into the batter. (This helps the batter stick beautifully!)
  5. Dip each piece into the batter and carefully place it into the hot oil. Fry in small batches — don’t overcrowd the pot!
  6. Fry for about 2-3 minutes, flipping once, until pale golden and crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  7. Repeat with the remaining ingredients.
  8. Serve immediately with dipping sauce on the side. Crunchy. Light. Heavenly.
