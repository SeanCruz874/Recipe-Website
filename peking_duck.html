Peking Duck

Peking Duck is a world-renowned Chinese dish featuring crispy, lacquered duck skin and tender meat, served with thin pancakes, hoisin sauce, and julienned vegetables. It’s celebrated for its elegant preparation and balance of flavors and textures.

Preparation:

24 hours (for marinating and drying)

Cook:

60–90 minutes

Total:

About 26 hours (including rest time)

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole duck (around 2.5–3 kg or 5–6 lbs)
  • 1 tablespoon maltose or honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for cavity)
  • 4 slices fresh ginger (for cavity)
  • To serve:
  • Mandarin pancakes or thin wheat wraps
  • Hoisin sauce or sweet bean sauce
  • Cucumber, julienned
  • Scallions, julienned

Instructions:

  1. Clean the duck thoroughly and pat dry. Remove excess fat from cavity. Insert green onions and ginger inside the duck.
  2. Bring a pot of water to a boil and pour it over the duck to tighten the skin. Pat dry again.
  3. Combine vinegar, maltose (or honey), and 1/2 cup hot water. Brush the mixture all over the duck skin.
  4. Hang or place the duck uncovered in a cool, airy place (or in the fridge) for 12–24 hours to dry the skin.
  5. Preheat oven to 190°C (375°F). Roast the duck breast-side-up on a rack over a pan of water for 45 minutes. Flip and roast another 30–45 minutes until skin is crisp and brown.
  6. Let duck rest 10 minutes. Carve thin slices, including crispy skin and meat.
  7. To serve: Spread hoisin sauce on a pancake, add duck slices, cucumber, and scallions. Roll and enjoy!
