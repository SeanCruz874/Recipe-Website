Tacos al Pastor

Tacos al Pastor are a Mexican street food favorite made with thinly sliced pork marinated in a flavorful blend of chilies, spices, and pineapple, traditionally cooked on a vertical spit. Served in warm corn tortillas with onions, cilantro, and fresh pineapple, they’re bold, juicy, and incredibly satisfying.

Preparation:

30 minutes (plus marinating time)

Cook:

30 minutes

Total:

1 hour (plus marinating)

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (900g) pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 3 dried guajillo chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons achiote paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned)
  • Corn tortillas, for serving
  • Chopped white onion, cilantro, lime wedges (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Soak guajillo chilies in hot water for 10–15 minutes until softened. Drain.
  2. In a blender, combine softened chilies, garlic, vinegar, orange juice, achiote paste, cumin, oregano, cloves, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth to make the marinade.
  3. In a bowl or zip-top bag, coat the sliced pork with the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  4. Grill the marinated pork (and pineapple slices if desired) over medium-high heat until cooked and slightly charred, about 4–6 minutes per side.
  5. Chop the grilled pork and pineapple into bite-sized pieces.
  6. Warm corn tortillas on a skillet or grill. Fill each tortilla with pork, pineapple, chopped onion, and cilantro.
  7. Serve hot with lime wedges on the side.
