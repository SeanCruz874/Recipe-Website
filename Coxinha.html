Coxinha

Golden, crispy Brazilian chicken croquettes shaped like little drumsticks — the ultimate savory snack for any party or cheat day!

Preparation:

40 minutes

Cook:

30 minutes

Total:

About 1 hour 10 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups (about 250g) cooked, shredded chicken breast
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cream cheese (optional, for extra creamy filling)
  • 2 cups (480ml) chicken broth
  • 2 cups (250g) all-purpose flour
  • Bread crumbs (for coating)
  • 2 eggs, beaten (for coating)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, sauté the onion and garlic in olive oil until soft and fragrant.
  2. Add the shredded chicken, season with salt and pepper, and mix well. Stir in cream cheese if using for extra yumminess. Set aside to cool.
  3. In a pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil. Once boiling, add the flour all at once, stirring hard until it forms a dough that pulls away from the sides of the pot.
  4. Let the dough cool slightly, then knead it until smooth.
  5. Take a small piece of dough, flatten it in your hand, place some chicken filling in the center, and shape it into a little teardrop or drumstick shape.
  6. Dip each coxinha into the beaten eggs, then coat in breadcrumbs.
  7. Heat oil in a deep fryer or deep pan. Fry the coxinhas in batches until golden brown and crispy (about 3–5 minutes).
  8. Drain on paper towels and serve hot!

  1. Season the fish chunks with lime juice, salt, and pepper. Let it marinate while you prep the veggies.
  2. In a large deep skillet or pot, heat the oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the onions, bell peppers, and garlic, and cook until they start to soften (about 5 minutes).
  4. Layer the tomatoes over the veggies, then gently place the fish pieces on top.
  5. Pour the coconut milk evenly over everything. If you like it spicy, throw in some sliced chili pepper here too!
  6. Cover and let it simmer gently for about 20-25 minutes, until the fish is cooked through and tender (don’t stir too much or the fish will break apart!).
  7. Sprinkle with lots of chopped cilantro before serving.
  8. Serve hot, usually with rice and maybe some farofa on the side!
