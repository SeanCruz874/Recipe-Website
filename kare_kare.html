Kare-Kare

Rich, nutty, and deeply comforting — Kare-Kare is a traditional Filipino peanut stew, often made with oxtail, vegetables, and a creamy peanut sauce, perfect for festive meals or hearty gatherings.

Preparation:

20-30 minutes active prep

Cook:

2-2.5 hours (mostly simmering)

Total:

About 2.5-3 hours

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds (900g) oxtail, cut into pieces (can substitute beef shank or tripes)
  • 8 cups (2 liters) water
  • 1 bundle bok choy or pechay
  • 1 banana blossom (optional)
  • 1 eggplant, sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter (creamy or chunky)
  • 1/4 cup ground toasted rice (or rice flour)
  • 1 tablespoon annatto seeds (atsuete) soaked in 1/4 cup warm water (or 1 tablespoon annatto powder)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooking oil
  • Bagoong alamang (fermented shrimp paste), for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, boil the oxtail in water until tender, about 2 hours. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface. Reserve the broth.
  2. In a separate small pan, heat the annatto seeds in oil (or use annatto powder). Strain and set the colored oil aside.
  3. In a large pan or pot, sauté onion and garlic in the annatto oil until fragrant.
  4. Add the tender oxtail and stir to coat in the oil and aromatics.
  5. Pour in about 4-5 cups of the reserved broth. Simmer gently.
  6. Stir in the peanut butter and mix until well blended. Add the ground toasted rice to thicken the sauce. Simmer until the sauce is rich and creamy.
  7. Separately, blanch the eggplant, green beans, banana blossom, and bok choy in boiling water until just tender. Set aside.
  8. Arrange the cooked vegetables on top of the kare-kare, or serve them on the side.
  9. Season with salt and pepper as needed. Serve hot with bagoong alamang on the side.
