Pad Thai

Pad Thai is Thailand’s iconic stir-fried noodle dish, combining chewy rice noodles, tangy tamarind sauce, crisp vegetables, and tender protein—topped with crunchy peanuts and fresh lime. It's the perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and savory.

Preparation:

15–20 minutes

Cook:

10–15 minutes

Total:

30–35 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces (225g) flat rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons oil (vegetable or peanut)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 8 ounces (225g) shrimp, chicken, or tofu
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro (optional)
  • Lime wedges, for serving

For the sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian)
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (palm sugar preferred)
  • 1–2 teaspoons chili flakes (optional, to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 20–30 minutes, or until pliable. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the fish sauce, tamarind paste, sugar, and chili flakes. Set the sauce aside.
  3. Heat oil in a wok or large pan over medium-high heat. Sauté garlic and shallots until fragrant.
  4. Add shrimp, chicken, or tofu and stir-fry until cooked through.
  5. Push everything to the side, pour in the eggs, and scramble them gently.
  6. Add the drained noodles and pour in the prepared sauce. Toss everything together until evenly coated and the noodles are tender (add a splash of water if needed).
  7. Stir in bean sprouts and green onions. Cook for 1–2 more minutes.
  8. Plate the Pad Thai and top with crushed peanuts, cilantro (if using), and lime wedges.
