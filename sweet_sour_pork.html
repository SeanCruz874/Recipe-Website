Sweet and Sour Pork (Tangsuyuk)

Tangsuyuk is a Korean-Chinese dish made with crispy deep-fried pork coated in a glossy sweet and sour sauce. With a balance of tangy, fruity, and savory flavors, it's typically served with stir-fried vegetables and sometimes pineapple.

Preparation:

20–25 minutes

Cook:

20 minutes

Total:

40–45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 400g (14 oz) pork loin or shoulder, cut into bite-size strips
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 egg white
  • 1/2 cup potato starch (or cornstarch)
  • Vegetable oil for deep-frying
  • Vegetables (optional):
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1/2 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 bell pepper, sliced
  • Pineapple chunks (optional)
  • Sweet and Sour Sauce:
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 3 tablespoons vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup (optional, for a fruity kick)
  • 1 tablespoon potato starch (mixed with 2 tbsp water for slurry)

Instructions:

  1. Season pork with salt and pepper. Add egg white and mix well. Coat pork evenly with potato starch.
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan to 170–180°C (340–355°F). Deep-fry pork pieces in batches until crispy and golden. For extra crunch, double-fry: rest for a few minutes and fry again quickly.
  3. In a separate pan, sauté vegetables briefly (if using). Set aside.
  4. To make the sauce, combine sugar, vinegar, soy sauce, water, and ketchup (if using) in a saucepan. Bring to a simmer.
  5. Stir in starch slurry and cook until the sauce thickens and turns glossy.
  6. Add vegetables and pineapple to the sauce and toss briefly to coat.
  7. Serve crispy pork on a plate and pour the sauce over just before eating, or serve sauce separately for dipping.
