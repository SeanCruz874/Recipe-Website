Sushi

Fresh, flavorful, and surprisingly fun to make — roll your own sushi right at home and feel like a master chef!

Preparation:

30-40 minutes active prep

Rice Cook/Chill Time:

~1 hour

Assemble:

20-30 minutes

Total:

About 2 hours (but it’s honestly so satisfying!)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups (400g) sushi rice
  • 2 1/2 cups (600ml) water
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Nori (seaweed sheets)
  • Fillings: sliced raw fish (like salmon or tuna — sushi-grade!), cucumber, avocado, crab sticks, etc.
  • Soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger (for serving)
  • Optional: sesame seeds, spicy mayo

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the sushi rice in cold water several times until the water runs mostly clear. Drain.
  2. In a pot or rice cooker, cook the rice with the 2 1/2 cups water according to package instructions (usually about 15-20 minutes).
  3. While the rice cooks, mix the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small bowl until dissolved.
  4. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a wide bowl (a wooden one if you have it!) and gently fold in the vinegar mixture. Cool the rice to room temperature.
  5. Place a sheet of nori shiny side down on a bamboo mat (or just a piece of parchment paper if you don't have a mat).
  6. Wet your fingers (to keep rice from sticking!) and spread a thin layer of rice over the nori, leaving about 1 inch uncovered at the top.
  7. Add your fillings horizontally across the bottom third of the rice. Don’t overstuff — less is more!
  8. Using the mat, roll the sushi tightly away from you, pressing gently to form a nice firm log.
  9. Seal the edge by wetting the bare strip of nori with a little water, then finish rolling.
  10. Use a sharp knife dipped in water to slice the roll into 6-8 pieces. (Don't squish your beautiful sushi!)
  11. Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger on the side. Maybe even sprinkle with sesame seeds or drizzle spicy mayo if you’re feeling extra!
