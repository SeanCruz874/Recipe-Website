Som Tum

Som Tum is a vibrant and spicy Thai green papaya salad made with shredded unripe papaya, lime juice, chili, fish sauce, and peanuts. It’s sweet, sour, salty, and crunchy — a refreshing side or light main dish packed with bold Southeast Asian flavors.

Preparation:

15 minutes

Cook:

None

Total:

15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded green papaya
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned (optional)
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup green beans, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons roasted peanuts, crushed
  • 2–3 cloves garlic
  • 2–3 Thai bird’s eye chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1–2 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian)
  • 1–2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a mortar and pestle, pound the garlic and chilies together to form a rough paste.
  2. Add sugar, lime juice, and fish sauce. Mix well until sugar is dissolved.
  3. Add the green beans and lightly crush them with the pestle.
  4. Add shredded papaya, tomatoes, and carrots (if using). Gently pound and mix everything together until combined and coated with the dressing.
  5. Stir in crushed peanuts.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with more lime juice, sugar, or fish sauce as desired.
  7. Serve immediately as a fresh, crunchy salad or side dish. Pairs well with sticky rice and grilled meats.
