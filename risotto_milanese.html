Risotto alla Milanese

Risotto alla Milanese is a luxurious Italian rice dish renowned for its golden color from saffron and its rich, creamy texture. This classic from Milan pairs wonderfully with ossobuco or stands beautifully on its own.

Preparation:

10–15 minutes active prep

Cook:

20–25 minutes

Total:

About 35–40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (divided)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups hot beef or chicken broth (kept warm)
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, steep saffron threads in 2 tablespoons of hot broth and set aside.
  2. In a wide pan, heat 1 tablespoon butter and the olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the chopped onion until translucent and soft, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the Arborio rice and stir to coat each grain with the butter and oil. Toast the rice for 2–3 minutes, stirring frequently.
  4. Pour in the white wine and stir until mostly absorbed.
  5. Begin adding the warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring constantly and allowing each addition to absorb before adding the next. This process takes about 18–20 minutes.
  6. Halfway through cooking, stir in the saffron-infused broth for color and flavor.
  7. When the rice is al dente and creamy, remove from heat. Stir in the remaining tablespoon of butter and the grated Parmesan. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve immediately, garnished with extra Parmesan if desired.
