Feijoada

A rich, savory Brazilian stew made with black beans, pork, and beef — a comforting and flavorful dish perfect for a feast!

Preparation:

30 minutes

Cook:

2 to 3 hours

Total:

About 3 to 3.5 hours

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups (400g) dried black beans, soaked overnight
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 300g pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 200g chorizo or smoked sausage, sliced
  • 150g bacon, chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: pork ribs, beef jerky, or other cured meats
  • Water or broth, enough to cover the beans and meats

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the soaked beans. Set aside.
  2. In a large heavy pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté onion and garlic until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the bacon and cook until it starts to crisp up.
  4. Stir in the pork shoulder chunks and sausages, browning them lightly.
  5. Add the drained beans to the pot, along with bay leaves and enough water (or broth) to fully cover everything.
  6. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer uncovered for about 2–3 hours, stirring occasionally and adding more water as needed.
  7. Season with salt and pepper near the end of cooking. The stew should be thick and the meats super tender.
  8. Serve hot, typically with rice, collard greens, orange slices, and maybe some farofa (toasted cassava flour) on the side!
