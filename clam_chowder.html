Clam Chowder

Creamy and comforting, Clam Chowder is a hearty soup made with clams, potatoes, onions, and bacon. This New England classic is perfect for chilly days and is often served with oyster crackers or crusty bread.

Preparation:

15–20 minutes

Cook:

30–35 minutes

Total:

45–55 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups (480ml) clam juice or broth
  • 1 cup (240ml) water or milk (adjust to desired consistency)
  • 2 cups potatoes, peeled and diced (Yukon Gold or Russet)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 (6.5 oz or ~185g) can chopped clams, drained (reserve juice)
  • 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley or chives, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, cook bacon over medium heat until crisp. Remove and set aside, leaving the drippings in the pot.
  2. Add butter, then sauté onions until soft, about 5 minutes. Stir in garlic and cook for 1 minute more.
  3. Sprinkle in flour and cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly to form a roux.
  4. Gradually whisk in clam juice, reserved clam liquid, and water or milk. Stir until smooth.
  5. Add diced potatoes, bay leaf, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15–20 minutes or until potatoes are tender.
  6. Stir in chopped clams and heavy cream. Simmer for another 5 minutes without boiling. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Remove bay leaf. Serve hot, garnished with bacon and fresh herbs.
